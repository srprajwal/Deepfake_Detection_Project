Our Approach

We presented a neural network-based approach to classify the video as deep fake or real, along with the confidence of the proposed model. The proposed method is inspired by the way deep fakes are created by GANs with the help of Autoencoders. Our method performs frame-level detection using ResNext CNN and video classification using RNN along with LSTM. This enables us to analyze both spatial and temporal features effectively. The proposed method is capable of detecting the video as a deep fake or real based on the listed parameters in our paper. We believe that it will provide very high accuracy on real-time data.

Why Deepfake Detection Matters

Deepfake technology has evolved rapidly, enabling the creation of hyper-realistic fake videos that are nearly indistinguishable from reality. These videos have been used for various malicious purposes, such as misinformation campaigns, identity theft, and even fraud. Detecting deepfakes is essential for maintaining trust in digital content, ensuring cybersecurity, and protecting individuals from being misrepresented or defamed.

Advanced Techniques in Detection

Our system incorporates cutting-edge technologies such as transfer learning and attention mechanisms to enhance detection accuracy. By leveraging pre-trained models like ResNet and fine-tuning them for deepfake-specific tasks, we achieve high precision in identifying subtle artifacts left by manipulation tools. Additionally, our use of LSTM layers for temporal analysis ensures robust detection for even highly dynamic videos.

The Future of Deepfake Detection

As deepfake generation methods become more sophisticated, detection algorithms must evolve to stay ahead. The future of deepfake detection lies in integrating AI-powered tools into accessible platforms, enabling real-time analysis of videos uploaded on social media and other platforms. Collaborative efforts between AI researchers, governments, and tech companies will play a critical role in combating this emerging threat.
